    • What is Overpopulation?



    The Earth's population has rapidly been increasing from time to time, and it deeply affects human geography in our world.
    Overpopulation is when an area has too many people to have enough living space. Many communities in today's world face overpopulation.

    Let's start off with a question:

    Which of the following is an example of a population?

    • Humans
    • Aliens
    • Toys



    Perfect! Now, let's discuss the factors that can affect the growth of populations:

    First off, we have got the birth rate (births per year). This is the biggest factor that contributes to our world's overpopulation.

    Second, we have got the death rate (deaths per year). This factor reduces overpopulation.

    Third, we have got immigration (immigrants per year). Immigration doesn't increase the world's population, but increases the population density of certain areas.

    Lastly, we have got emigration (emigrants per year). Emigration also doesn't decrease the world's population, but it decreases the population density of an area.
